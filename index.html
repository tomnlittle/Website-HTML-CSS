About

I have been intensely interested in computers and engineering from a young age, and have competed in several computer science and engineering competitions throughout my life.
I first began to experiment with basic programming or scripting around the ages of 11 and 12, where I started to write mini programs in Bash and the windows command line. By age 12 I had started to learn Ruby and continued to work with it throughout high school.
At the age of 15 I began to experiment with JAVA, and the realm of object oriented programming. With the aid of Minecraft, I furthered my abilities by creating a considerable modification to the game, adding in my own characters, AI, environments and weapons.
In High School, as part of the curriculum I studied python and HTML/CSS. I competed in a number of Computing Competitions and scored highly in the NCSS challenge.
I am currently working on a couple of projects, such as a drone controlled from Raspberry Pi. I found during my first year of University that COMP2121 greatly interested me, it was a course which mainly focused on microcontrollers and how they are used; it covered in depth assembly programming, interrupts and control systems. In Semester 1 of 2017, I will continue my studies at UNSW, where I have enrolled in the Artificial Intelligence, Operating Systems, and Computing for Engineers Courses; as well as Discrete Maths.
